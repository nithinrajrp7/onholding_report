Executive Summary

ON Holding's 2025 guidance (≥CHF 2.94bn sales at ≥27% reported growth, ~60.5% gross margin) embeds resilience to FX and tariff pressures, but analyst concerns center on margin sustainability, working capital control, pricing limits, and growth realism. My base case delivers EBIT of CHF 286m (9.7% margin, +60bps YoY) via SG&A leverage; the upside scenario reaches CHF 343m (11.5%) with meaningful tariff mitigation; the worst case flattens at CHF 210m (7.5%), still demonstrating topline resilience under adverse macro conditions.

FX Deep-Dive: SG&A Analysis

SG&A increased from CHF 887m in 2023 to CHF 1,194m in 2024 (+34.6%), outpacing reported net sales growth of +29.4%. As a result, the SG&A ratio rose by 200bps year-on-year, from 49.5% to 51.5% of net sales. At first glance this suggests limited operating leverage; however, a closer look reveals a more nuanced picture.

Adjusting for constant-currency sales, the reported SG&A ratio overstates the underlying cost intensity. ON's net sales grew 33.2% in constant currency in 2024, implying FX-neutral revenue of approximately CHF 2.39bn versus the reported CHF 2.32bn. On this FX-neutral revenue base, the SG&A ratio would have been approximately 50.0% — only ~50bps above the 2023 level, rather than the 200bps deterioration implied by reported numbers.

FX Translation Effect Approximately 150bps of the year-on-year SG&A ratio increase reflects FX translation effects on the top line. The remaining ~50bps is driven by underlying mix and cost decisions — primarily higher marketing spend and share-based compensation.

Within SG&A, ON has begun to demonstrate scale benefits in its logistics and sales platform. Distribution and selling expenses declined to 12.4% and 7.3% of net sales respectively in 2024, down 100bps and 10bps year-on-year. At the same time, the company deliberately stepped up brand investment: marketing expenses increased by 41.3% year-on-year (+100bps to 11.9% of net sales), G&A grew 35.9% (+80bps to 16.8% of sales), and share-based compensation more than doubled to CHF 71.5m, adding approximately 130bps to the SG&A ratio.

Summary: FX Effect on SG&A

On a reported basis, SG&A increased from 49.5% to 51.5% of net sales in 2024. Using management's constant-currency net sales disclosure as the denominator, the same SG&A expense corresponds to a ratio of roughly 50.0%. This constant-currency lens suggests that approximately three-quarters of the apparent SG&A ratio increase is attributable to FX-driven denominator effects, with the remaining one-quarter driven by underlying changes in cost intensity — notably higher marketing and share-based compensation. This is an illustrative diagnostic rather than a full FX remeasurement of SG&A, which also contains foreign-currency-denominated costs.

05

FX Impact on Cash & Cash Equivalents

Cash generated from operations more than doubled to CHF 510.6m in 2024 (from CHF 232.1m in 2023), underpinning a strong improvement in underlying free cash flow. The total change in net cash and cash equivalents for 2024 was CHF 390.4m, which — added to the opening balance of CHF 494.6m — brought reported year-end cash to CHF 924.3m.

However, embedded within this increase is a CHF 39.4m FX translation gain on non-CHF cash balances (versus a CHF 39.6m FX translation loss in 2023). This line item represents the mechanical effect of translating ON's non-CHF cash holdings into Swiss Francs at a stronger closing rate — it is not the product of operating performance. Stripping out this CHF 39.4m non-operational gain yields a normalised year-end cash position of CHF 885m (CHF 924.3m − CHF 39.4m). I use this normalised figure as the sustainable cash balance for valuation purposes, given that the FX translation effect on cash is inherently volatile and reverses with exchange rate movements, as the 2023 swing of −CHF 39.6m illustrates.

06

Tariff Impact Assessment

ON sources the majority of its products from Vietnam and Indonesia, which face effective duty rates of approximately 19–20% — a portion of which ON was already incurring pre-2025 via existing duties and fees. The incremental exposure from new US import tariffs introduces an estimated gross margin headwind of approximately 150–200bps pre-mitigation. ON's Lightspray manufacturing technology and ongoing supply chain optimization initiatives provide partial cost offsets; COGS as a percentage of sales improved materially in 2024 (from CHF 724m to CHF 913m on a CHF 526m sales increase), and this trajectory informs the mitigation assumptions embedded in each scenario.

Valuation

Cash Quality & Normalization Note

ON's reported year-end 2024 cash stands at CHF 924.3m. However, embedded within the headline cash balance is a CHF 39.4m FX translation gain — the mechanical effect of translating non-CHF cash holdings into Swiss Francs at a more favourable closing rate. This item is volatile by nature: the equivalent FX translation line was −CHF 39.6m in 2023. Stripping this out yields a normalised cash balance of CHF 885m (CHF 924.3m − CHF 39.4m), which I use as the primary net cash figure in all valuation scenarios. Debt is negligible (~0), so normalised net cash equals normalised gross cash.

Primary Analysis — Fixed 34x EV/EBIT Multiple

Market-implied EV/EBIT of ~34x (reference: AlphaSpread TTM as of October 2025, EV ~$13.9bn USD ÷ EBIT CHF 348m at average 2025 FX).
